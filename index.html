About

I work at the intersection of design and social impact — building programs and moments that help people encounter a different perspective on themselves and their potential. My approach starts from what already exists: the people, tensions, and unmet needs in a room, rather than an idea I want to force onto reality. I care about the difference between giving people information and creating the conditions where understanding can actually land.

That thinking shows up in Social Impactathon, in the leadership programs I've run in Taiwan and the U.S., and in a TEDx talk I gave on navigating life's uncertainties. You can read more about my path from Taiwan to USC Marshall in this USC Marshall feature.
